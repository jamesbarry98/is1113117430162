Irish Coast Guard

The overall objective of the Coast Guard is to reduce the loss of life on Irish seas, lakes, waterways and rivers, coastal and remote areas. The main rescues are done by the rescue helicopters located in Shannon, Dublin, Waterford and Sligo. Throughout the country there are stations which operate rescues in their local areas. These units engage in cliff rescues, shore rescues and on water rescues.

Im an active member of the Old Head Seven Heads Coast Guard Unit. This is both a cliff, shore and sea unit. The unit has a D-Class rescue boat as well advanced cliff rescue equipment and vehicles. Our unit trains regularly and is capable of caring out a variety of rescues. Members are trained in survival, search, radio operation and first aid with resuscitation.

At the manned radio stations,there is 24-hour monitoring so emergency calls are responded to as quickly as possible. The IRCG maintains thorough information on the location of ships and boats. Many ships are now equipped with automatic location equipment transmitting information about their location at regular intervals. This means that their whereabouts can be monitored on the screen at the Marine Rescue Centres. There are 3 manned radio stations in Ireland located in Malin Head, Valentia and Dublin.
